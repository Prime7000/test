Probability is a branch of mathematics that deals with the likelihood of events. It is expressed as a number in the range from 0 to 1, or, using percentage notation, in the range from 0% to 100%. The more likely it is that the event will occur, the higher its probability. One example of a probability problem is the following:
What is the probability of flipping a coin and getting heads?
There are two possible outcomes when flipping a coin: heads or tails. Since heads is one of the two possible outcomes, the probability of getting heads is 1/2 or 50%. Another example of a probability problem is the following:
What is the probability of rolling a 6 on a standard die?
There are 6 possible outcomes when rolling a die: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, or 6. Since 6 is one of the possible outcomes, the probability of rolling a 6 is 1/6 or 16.67%. Probability is used in many different fields, including statistics, gambling, and risk assessment. It is a powerful tool for making predictions about the future.
